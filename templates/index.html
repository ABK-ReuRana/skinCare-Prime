Basal cell carcinoma is a common variant of epithelial skin cancer that rarely metastasizes but grows destructively if untreated. It appears in different morphologic variants (flat, nodular, pigmented, cystic, etc).

Prevention and control

  • Sun protection: Avoid prolonged sun exposure and protect your skin from harmful UV rays by wearing protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses. Also, apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher every two hours.
  • Quit smoking: Smoking can damage your skin and increase your risk of developing basal cell carcinoma. Quitting smoking can lower your risk and improve your overall health.
  • Seek medical attention: If you notice any suspicious spots or growths on your skin, seek medical attention promptly. Your doctor can perform a biopsy and determine if it is cancerous. Early detection and treatment can prevent the cancer from spreading and improve your chances of a full recovery.

Dermatofibroma is a common benign skin growth that usually occurs on the legs of adults. While it is not typically a serious condition, it can cause discomfort and cosmetic concern.

Prevention and control

  • Keep your skin healthy: Good skin health is important for preventing dermatofibromas. This includes keeping your skin clean and moisturized, avoiding harsh soaps and other skin irritants, and maintaining a healthy diet and lifestyle.
  • Treat existing dermatofibromas: If you already have dermatofibromas, there are a few options for treatment. In some cases, the growths can be removed surgically. Other treatments may include cryotherapy (freezing the growths) or laser therapy.
  • See a dermatologist: If you are concerned about dermatofibromas or notice any unusual growths on your skin, it's important to see a dermatologist for an evaluation. A dermatologist can help diagnose the condition and provide treatment options.

Vesicular skin lesions are nothing but blisters. They are small sacs filled with either clear or pus-colored fluid. They are typically 5-10mm in diameter. They are delicate and can burst due to pressure or friction. When a vesicle bursts, it becomes a vesicular eruption. Once they burst, the liquid spreads to the surrounding skin and leaves a white or yellow flaky crust when it dries.
